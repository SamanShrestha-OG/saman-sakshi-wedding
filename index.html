Our Story

His Side — Saman

September 2021. Our best friends were in the middle of a dramatic breakup — the kind with season-finale energy — and meanwhile I was just trying to mind my business.

Then I saw her. My entire brain shut down and rebooted with one single thought: “Damn!! Ckwaaak.” Don’t ask me why — my heart spoke before my mouth could catch up.

I came that day to listen to my friend vent, maybe grab snacks, go home… normal human things. Instead, I saw Sakshi and immediately forgot how to act. My posture improved, my voice got deeper, and I started saying things like “yeah, I’ve been going to the gym.” (Spoiler: I was not.)

And then she laughed — and I thought, “Yup. That’s it. I’m done for.” She was funny, chaotic, gorgeous, dramatic and calm all at once — basically a walking plot twist… and I was hooked.

And now, after all the twists, airport goodbyes, random city moves, dramatic friend breakups, and one unforgettable “Ckwaaak” moment… here we are, about to start forever.

If our story were a movie, this is where the music swells and everyone whispers, “Ugh… they’re so cute.” And they’d be right — because somehow, against all odds and all zip codes, we found our happily-ever-after anyway.

Her Side — Sakshi

What was supposed to be a simple getaway in 2021 turned into… destiny being chaotic but efficient.

At the time, I was basically living the nomad life — New Jersey → New York → Austin → Pocatello — collecting zip codes like they were loyalty points.

Then came Dallas. Not because of the weather, or the traffic, or the Texas heat that tries to roast you alive every summer… but because of Saman.

He made the chaos feel lighter. He made everyday moments feel like they were exactly where I was supposed to be. He made “home” feel less like a place and more like a person.

I wasn’t planning to fall in love — but then he said “Hey,” and the whole world rearranged itself.

Through every move, every laugh, and every long-distance moment, he was the constant I didn’t know I needed. And after all this time, I finally stopped running from place to place…

Because I found where I’m meant to stay.
